The Psychology of Computer Programming

By Gerald M. Weinberg

1971

This classic book explores the human aspects of programming and software development, examining the psychological and social elements that impact programmers and the quality of their work. Weinberg emphasizes the need to consider both the technical and emotional aspects of programming, encouraging developers to think about their habits, motivations, and interactions with colleagues.

The book dives into topics such as team dynamics, problem-solving, productivity, and the role of management in fostering a productive programming environment.
